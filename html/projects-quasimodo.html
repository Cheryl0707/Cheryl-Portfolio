Quasimodo

A Bluetooth speaker provides an immersive acoustic experience and excellent aesthetic experience inspired by the Notre Dame de Paris in Victor Hugo’s novel. (SFU Fall 2022 Project Showcase)

- Project Type: Material Design / Product Design

- Team member: Kejing Ren, Ruijie Liu, Zhengji Li, Yuting Hu

- Roles: Sketch / Laser cutting pattern design / UI design / physical Model Assemble and Refinement

- Date: April - July, 2023

- Tools: Solid works, Figma, Foam Cutter, Plaster

Project Description

We were inspired by the Notre Dame de Paris and the Victor Hugo novel of the same name to create a bluetooth speaker with a distinctive gothic style modeled after the cathedral. We incorporated the pointed arches, stained-glasses windows, ribbed vaults, and spires into the speaker form and designed the speaker to provide an immersive acoustic experience and excellent aesthetic experience for the user. The main Bluetooth speaker is stored in the middle under the engraved wood panel and users can remotely control the speaker with digital devices. We aimed to target the artist community to set the user context in the artist’s studio, where they stay for a long time. The cathedral speaker will help them immersively create art pieces. And users can remotely control the speaker with digital devices.

Inspiration

This project is inspired by Gothic architectures which are represented with a distinctive gothic style, especially the pointed arches, stained-glasses windows, ribbed vaults and spires.

However, on April 15, 2019, the wooden roof was heavily damaged by fire. “The eyes of the whole world then watch, helplessly, what could become the direct destruction of one of the greatest jewels of humanity's heritage.”

Metaphor: We also inspired by the protagonist of novel Notre Dame de Paris, Quasimodo, who born deformed, possessing a hunched back. He is the bell ringer of Notre Dame de Paris. We believe the bell ringing brings to the cathedral of Notre Dame in 1482, where he leaved for his whole life and forbidden to leave.

Ideation Sketches

With these special themes in mind, our goal is to create a Bluetooth speaker that offers users a rich visual and acoustical listening experience. We extracted Gothic design features and blended them into functional speakers.

Solid Works

Prepare Materials

We use Walnut wood and Plaster as the main material. Walnut wood is used to create panels in the middle, and laser cutting patterns on the surface to have more decorative appearance. It also reflecting sound waves coming from the speaker, providing better acoustic experiences. Plaster is used to create pillars and arches, referring to gothic architectures and the concepts of religions, which adds the feeling of modernity.
