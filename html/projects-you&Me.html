What is Virtual Production

Virtual Production (aka In-Camera Visual Effects) is a cutting-edge technology combines physical and virtual filmmaking techniques to create cutting-edge media. Utilizes LED panels as dynamic backdrops for sets, and create visual effects through camera lens. This methods helps us render the 3D virtual world through the camera’s perspective.

Compare to the traditional production...

Traditional VP

Advantages:
- Have mature production workflow and resources
- Have standard production tools and techniques

Drawbacks:
- Need heavy VFX for impossible environment
- Lengthy post-production execution time
- High costs

Emerging VP

Advantages:
- Realistic environments assets can move around
- Real-time render
- Flexible production location and time
- Open engine: good for indies to experiment more practical techniques and effects

Drawbacks:
- Needs more professionals to do the compositing to polish the final
- Completely new workflow, with potentials and uncertainties.

Emerging Virtual Production divided into three main steps:

1: Build the digital set

in unreal engine. Normally the bigger (all-around) virtual world would be better with more flexibility.

2: Character performing and recording.

Consider to choose completely virtual or hybrid production. Complete virtual production need motion and face capture, retargeting the animation to virtual character, and may need clean the motion data afterwards. Hybrid virtual production which mixed real performers and virtual environment, professional unreal operator and production team are necessary.

3: Polish the final presentation.

Involving assembling, colour grading & correction, add visual effects, and sound.
